TechZone Academy is a startup whose mission is to provide world class technological education, training and services in rural and remote areas. TechZone Academy was founded by Vinay Taragi, a young entrepreneur from Hawalbagh, Almora (Uttarakhand) and began with a single center in Kosi, Almora as an institute providing computer operation training to local youth as well as selling and servicing mobile and electronic consumer goods. Shortly after starting up, Vinay partnered with social entrepreneur and Ashoka fellow Arjun Venkatraman to expand the range and scope of the business. Today, in addition to computer operation training, the Kosi center also offers courses in programming, basic electronics, computer security, personality development programmes and also spoken English training. The range of services has grown to include tech support to local businesses and administrative bodies, web site development and maintenance, social media presence management and maintenance.

Since connectivity remains a concern in our area of operation, TechZone Academy also provides setup and maintenance services for local WiFi networks that can provide Internet access in places where commercial and government owned telecom providers have not been able to provide quality services. The WiFi program is called COWMesh (Community Owned WiFi Mesh) and the pilot commercial deployment has been active for approximately a year in villages Hawalbagh and Jyoli (also in Almora district). Since the pilot we have deployed a similar network in Maharashtra and are now working on building a pipeline of customers. Having established our fundamentals over the first year of operation, we are now keen to grow from a small, one store business into a sustainable social enterprise that delivers quality products and services while generating employment and skills in remote and rural areas.
